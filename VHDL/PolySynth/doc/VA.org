** How the octaves work
+ Each octave is a doubling of the frequency
+ The octave selector on the Moog has 4 values ranging from 2' to 16'
  - 2' would be the highest and it would be 2 octaves above 8' (1760 hz)
  - 16' would be one octave below 8' (220 hz)
  - 8' would be directly in the middle (440 hz)

* Math/Logic behind the synth
+ Before we start its key to remember the zed board DAC has a resolution of 24 bits 
  - Meaning a range of 0 to 16,777,215 
  - Meaning our bit depth needs to be at least 24 bits to match our dac resolution
+ Using this range we can generate the necessary value in a lut for our DAC
+ For our address spaces should be some complement of 2
  - for this dac resolution we want something higher than 10 that dosen't take up too much
    memory 
    * 16 bit should work for now and we can change it later
+ In order to change the pitch we just need to change the rate at which
   we step through our synth
** Setting up the Phase Increment {*Step Size*} 
+ This formula gives us the step size (*M*) for a specific frequency
  * \[
    M = \frac{f_{out} \cdot 2^{N}}{f_{s}}
    \]
    - Fs in this case would be our clock rate 
      - i.e how many samples we could take per second
